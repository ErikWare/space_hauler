# Mara System — Planet Lore & Visual Spec
*Space Hauler · World Bible · AI Art Generation Reference*

---

## The Mara System at a Glance

The Mara system is a fractured eight-world system locked in a cold war that the player slowly discovers was engineered by its oldest inhabitants. Three factions control the planets: the **Krag**, industrial scavengers who strip-mined their own worlds to survive a war they didn't start; the **Vex**, a rigid military civilization that responded to manufactured threats with manufactured force; and the **Nox**, the architects of everything — ancient, patient, and nearly invisible.

The player arrives believing the war is about resources. By the time they leave, they know it's about extinction — and the Nox aren't sure yet which species survives.

---

## The Three Factions

### KRAG
**Identity:** Working-class survivors. Industrial, rough, loyal to their own. They strip-mined their planets not out of greed but out of fear — the Vex were coming and they needed weapons, fast. Krag architecture is brutalist and functional: exposed pipes, rust-weathered metal, salvaged materials welded onto older salvaged materials. Their towns have a working-port feel — gritty but inhabited, warm in the windows at night.

**Visual language for AI art:** Rust-orange and iron-grey. Riveted plating. Stack chimneys. Squat, heavy structures. Characters wear thick industrial suits, goggles, heavy boots. Warm amber lamplight. Ore carts. Nothing decorative — everything is load-bearing.

**Palette:** `#8B4513` (rust brown) · `#696969` (iron grey) · `#FFA040` (amber lamp) · `#3D2B1F` (shadow) · `#C8A87A` (dust/weathered)

---

### VEX
**Identity:** Military order built on obedience and threat response. They genuinely believe they're protecting civilization. Their worlds are fortified, precise, cold. Vex architecture has no warmth — sharp edges, smooth black stone, red indicator lights. Their people wear sealed armor suits. They don't decorate. They fortify.

**Visual language for AI art:** Black basalt and chrome. Red tactical lighting. Sharp geometric architecture. Clean lines, no rust, no warmth. Military checkpoints, blast shields, sensor arrays. Soldiers in sealed black armor. Everything looks like a weapons system.

**Palette:** `#1A1A2E` (deep black-blue) · `#C0392B` (Vex red) · `#708090` (chrome/steel) · `#2C3E50` (shadow) · `#E8E8E8` (cold white trim)

---

### NOX
**Identity:** Ancient. Patient. Neither good nor evil — they operate on a timescale where individual wars are just experiments. They engineered the Krag-Vex conflict to reduce both populations to manageable levels. They live in near-darkness. Their architecture is organic and crystalline, grown rather than built. Their language is gesture and light. They are not hostile unless cornered — but they are never honest.

**Visual language for AI art:** Deep violet and bioluminescent teal. Crystalline spires. No straight lines. Cave-like organic structures. Glowing runes. Mist. Very little visible light — everything is ambient and sourceless. Figures are tall and thin, robed, faces hidden. Ancient ruin aesthetic crossed with living coral.

**Palette:** `#2D0040` (void purple) · `#00CED1` (bioluminescent teal) · `#7B2D8B` (violet) · `#0A0012` (near-black) · `#E0B4FF` (pale glow)

## The 8 Planets

---

### 1. MIRA
**Type:** Life World · **Faction:** Krag · **Orbit:** 44,000u (innermost habitable zone)

**Lore:**
Mira is the oldest Krag settlement and the planet the player calls home base. It was once covered in deep forests and rich farmland — the breadbasket of the system. Then the Vex warned the Krag that war was coming and the Krag believed them. Within a generation, the industrial districts had eaten the forests from the south. What remains is a patchwork: lush northern farmland that still grows, Krag working towns full of people who remember what it used to look like, and a southern industrial scar the locals call the Wound.

Elder Mira — the ancient NPC who lives on her farmhouse porch — knows the full truth: the Vex threat was fabricated. The Nox fed both sides false intelligence. The Krag strip-mined their paradise for a war the Nox needed them to fight.

**Surface feel:** Stardew Valley warmth in a world that knows it lost something. Green and alive in the north, rusted and quiet in the south. Krag towns are busy and lived-in. People here are not broken — they're working. They just don't talk about the Wound much.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, 16×16 tiles, Stardew Valley aesthetic
- Lush grass fields interrupted by dirt roads and small Krag towns
- Warm greens and browns, amber lamplight, rust-red industrial edges on the south end
- Crops visible in tilled fields: blue-green Mira Root, golden Star Grain
- Rivers cutting through farmland. Simple wooden fences. Barns with rust-patched roofs.

**Key palette:** `#5A9E4F` (grass) · `#4C8A42` (shadow grass) · `#7A5030` (soil) · `#B09870` (road) · `#3A8AB8` (water) · `#FFA040` (town lamps)

**Crops:** Mira Root (blue-green bulbs), Blue Kelp (aquatic, teal tendrils), Star Grain (golden stalks), Vault Spore (deep purple mushroom), Krag Fungus (grey-orange shelf mushroom)

**Notable structures:** Farmhouse (wood + stone, amber windows), Barn (corrugated metal roof, rust-red), Krag Town Hall (squat grey stone, faction flag), Seed Market (canvas awning, open-air), Industrial sheds (south edge — rusted corrugated walls, empty ore carts outside)

### 2. DUSK
**Type:** Ice World · **Faction:** Krag · **Orbit:** 72,000u

**Lore:**
Dusk was never supposed to be colonized. It sits at the outer edge of the habitable zone and temperatures drop to -80°C at night. The Krag settled it anyway because Dusk sits on one of the largest veins of platinum ore in the system. The miners who came here didn't leave. Three generations later, Dusk has a real culture: ice-fisher villages on the frozen lakes, underground heated warrens where families live, and a fatalistic pride in surviving what no one else would.

The Vex have never attacked Dusk. The Nox have, twice, quietly — testing cold-environment operations. Neither attack was acknowledged publicly. The miners know something happened to Survey Team 7. They don't ask where the bodies are.

**Surface feel:** Brutal and beautiful. A white world with deep blue shadows. Ice lake villages that glow amber through the blizzard. Underground warrens carved into glacier walls. The surface is sparse but the community below is tight and warm.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, icy tundra aesthetic
- Flat white snowfields broken by frozen dark-blue lakes
- Krag ice-fisher villages: low huts with heavy timber frames, orange-lit windows in snowdrifts
- Ice sheet surface with crack patterns (lighter blue lines on white)
- Underground Warren entrance: a carved archway in a glacier wall, warm light spilling out
- Platinum ore veins visible in cliff faces (silver-white mineral glitter)

**Key palette:** `#E8F4F8` (snow white) · `#6BA3BE` (ice blue) · `#2C3D4F` (frozen lake dark) · `#FFA040` (amber warmth) · `#C0C0C0` (platinum) · `#4A3728` (timber/Krag structure)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "Surface storm detected — landing suspended for 40 cycles." One Krag engineer stands outside a sealed blast door, checking sensors.

### 3. SORN
**Type:** Desert World · **Faction:** Krag · **Orbit:** 58,000u

**Lore:**
Sorn was strip-mined first and fastest — the Krag needed iron and silicon and Sorn had both in abundance. What's left is a dust bowl. The Krag salvagers who stayed aren't miners anymore — there's nothing left to mine. They're scavengers now, picking through the ruins of old extraction equipment for parts. Sorn towns are built from the bones of old industry: machine housings converted into walls, conveyor belts dismantled for flooring, crane arms welded into arches.

There is one thing Sorn still has that no one has taken: the Wind Archive. A network of caves in the western canyons where the constant wind has carved the rock walls into something the Krag miners called "scripture" — mile-long acoustic chambers that make sounds no instrument can reproduce. The Nox visit them sometimes. No one knows why.

**Surface feel:** Desolate post-extraction. The bones of industry slowly being buried by dust. Salvager camps that look temporary but have been there for decades. Hot orange light. Dust hanging in the air constantly. And in the far west, the ancient canyon walls that are somehow more alive than the town.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, desert salvagepunk aesthetic
- Vast sand/dust fields in pale orange and tan, with exposed rock formations
- Rusted industrial remnants half-buried in sand: conveyor joints, drill heads, pipe clusters
- Salvager settlement: structures built from machine parts, canvas tents, jury-rigged everything
- Canyon entrance to the west — deep red-orange rock walls, narrow shadows
- Wind-carved cave entrance: organic swirling shapes in stone, no Krag construction here

**Key palette:** `#C8A06A` (pale sand) · `#8B4513` (rust/iron) · `#D2691E` (orange rock) · `#4A3728` (shadow) · `#F0E68C` (dusty pale sky) · `#696969` (old metal)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "Dust storm season — 40-day surface blackout." A Krag salvager sheltering in a bunker entrance, goggles up, eating rations.

### 4. VESPER
**Type:** Cratered World · **Faction:** Vex · **Orbit:** 33,000u (close to star)

**Lore:**
Vesper is close to the star and airless — a heavily cratered rock that should be worthless. The Vex chose it precisely because it's visible from almost everywhere in the system. Vesper is a statement. A planet-sized fortress that says: *we are here, and we are watching.* The entire surface is a military installation. Weapons emplacements in the crater rims. Underground command bunkers that stretch kilometers down. Docking rings that can handle fleets. No civilians. No civilians have ever been on Vesper.

The player who lands on Vesper has made a mistake or a very deliberate choice. The Vex soldier at the checkpoint is polite. The soldier behind them is not.

**Surface feel:** Stark, bare, and deliberate. Craters used as defensive positions. Black angular Vex construction pressed into the terrain. No color except red warning lights and white flood beams. The silence of an airless world where everything communicates through screens.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, military sci-fi aesthetic
- Grey cratered terrain, craters with sharp shadows (close star = hard shadows)
- Vex military installations in the crater basins: black geometric structures, red indicator lights
- Surface texture: bare rock, grey dust, no vegetation anywhere, meteor impact scars
- Checkpoint structure: blast doors, sensor arrays, Vex insignia in red
- Weapon emplacements on crater rims: angular black turrets with red targeting lasers

**Key palette:** `#808080` (grey rock) · `#505050` (crater shadow) · `#1A1A2E` (Vex structure) · `#C0392B` (red tactical) · `#E8E8E8` (floodlight white) · `#2C2C2C` (deep shadow)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "Vex Military Exclusion Zone — all approach without clearance is a hostile act." A Vex soldier in full black sealed armor who won't move and won't explain.

### 5. CINDER
**Type:** Lava World · **Faction:** Vex · **Orbit:** 20,000u (very close to star)

**Lore:**
Cinder is the Vex answer to a hostile environment: bend it to purpose. The planet's surface is mostly molten, with stable islands of cooled basalt in the equatorial band. The Vex built forge-cities on these islands — massive industrial complexes where the lava itself is the energy source and the manufacturing medium. Cinder produces 60% of Vex weapons hardware. Every Vex ship in the system was partly born here.

The workers who run the forges are not slaves — the Vex are more subtle than that. They are workers who signed lifetime contracts for wages that never quite pay off the debts of the equipment rental, the hazard insurance, the housing in the forge-city towers. The Vex call it "structured employment." The workers call it the Burn.

**Surface feel:** Hot, orange, relentlessly industrial. Lava flows visible from the landing pad. The forge-city towers rise from basalt islands, connected by elevated walkways above the lava. Everything is smeared with ash. The air shimmer is visible even in the game's overhead view.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, volcanic industrial aesthetic
- Dark basalt ground with orange lava channels cutting between platforms
- Ash-grey surface dust, heat shimmer effect (slight pixel distortion near lava)
- Vex forge-city: sharp black geometric towers connected by elevated walkways/bridges
- Lava flows: animated if possible, bright orange-yellow core to dark red edge
- Industrial vents and exhaust stacks with orange glow at the top
- Small cooling pools of dark red rock where lava recently set

**Key palette:** `#FF6600` (lava bright) · `#CC3300` (lava edge) · `#1A1A1A` (basalt) · `#4A4A4A` (ash grey) · `#C0392B` (Vex red trim) · `#1A1A2E` (Vex structure)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "Volcanic activity — surface temperature 420°C — docking ring only." A Vex researcher in a white heat-resistant suit standing at the docking ring entrance, sweating through the suit.

### 6. ARIX
**Type:** Gas Giant · **Faction:** Vex · **Orbit:** 110,000u (outer system)

**Lore:**
Arix has no solid surface. The Vex don't need one. They built floating platforms in the upper atmosphere — research stations anchored to the cloud layer by tethers that go down five kilometers before they lose tension. The platforms study something the Vex have been tracking in the deep gas layer for ninety years: a signal. Rhythmic, mathematical, originating from below any depth they can probe. The Vex believe it's natural — electromagnetic resonance from the metallic hydrogen core. Three researchers have requested early termination of their contracts after spending more than two years on Arix. Two of them cited "irrational anxiety." One cited "mathematical certainty that something is listening."

The Nox know what the signal is. They haven't said.

**Surface feel:** Disorienting and alien. No ground — only platform edges and the swirling orange-tan gas below. The atmosphere is lit in massive bands of color from the gas layer. Vex research modules are white and sealed. The tether anchors are massive and ominous. Looking down is discouraged.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, floating platform / atmosphere aesthetic
- Platform surface: white Vex composite material, black grating sections, red safety rails
- Visible atmosphere below: swirling orange, tan, and ochre gas bands (visible between platforms)
- Tether anchor points: large hexagonal bolted fixtures, cables going straight down into gas
- Research modules: white sealed pods connected by enclosed walkways
- Atmospheric bands: wide and slow-moving, light orange to deep burnt amber
- Sky above: deep blue-black (outer atmosphere) with visible star field

**Key palette:** `#F5A623` (gas orange) · `#C8860A` (deep gas amber) · `#E8E8E8` (Vex platform white) · `#1A1A2E` (Vex structure) · `#C0392B` (red safety) · `#0A0A1E` (outer sky)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "No solid surface — docking ring access only — research personnel clearance required." A Vex platform worker in white coveralls, nervous, who says the platform "shifts" sometimes and she doesn't mean the wind.

### 7. HALVETH
**Type:** Purple Gas Giant · **Faction:** Nox · **Orbit:** 160,000u (deep outer system)

**Lore:**
Halveth is the first place you realize the Nox are not what you assumed. The planet is a gas giant but the Nox didn't build platforms — they grow them. Halveth's upper atmosphere contains biological structures kilometers across: membrane-sacs filled with lighter-than-gas compounds, attached to root networks that descend into the cloud layer. The Nox live inside these sacs. Their settlements look like organs more than buildings. Light pulses through the membrane walls.

The ritual grounds are real. The Nox conduct something here every 40-day cycle that they have never explained to any outsider. The only Krag who witnessed one — a trader who got lost in a gas storm and drifted into a sac structure — came back unable to sleep. She said the Nox weren't doing anything violent. She said they were counting. She wouldn't say what.

**Surface feel:** Alien and beautiful. Violet and deep purple gas, bioluminescent structures drifting in vast formations. No hard edges anywhere — everything curves. The light moves. Looking at it too long makes the player feel small in a way none of the other planets achieve.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, bioluminescent alien organic aesthetic
- Violet and deep purple gas layer with swirling lighter lavender bands
- Nox biological structures: large curved membrane-sac forms, glowing teal-green from within
- Root network visible below: branching dark purple tendrils disappearing into cloud
- Bioluminescent accents: teal and pale violet points of light scattered across structures
- Interior membrane walls: translucent purple skin with visible light pulsing through
- Ritual circle areas: open membrane floors, rings of teal light, minimal and geometric

**Key palette:** `#2D0040` (void purple) · `#7B2D8B` (gas violet) · `#B44FCC` (lighter purple band) · `#00CED1` (bioluminescent teal) · `#E0B4FF` (pale glow) · `#0A0012` (deep void)

**Stub reason (in-world):** "Nox quarantine protocols active — approach is a treaty violation." A Nox emissary stands at the docking membrane opening. It is taller than it should be. It doesn't say anything useful. It doesn't need to.

### 8. NOX PRIME
**Type:** Dark World · **Faction:** Nox · **Orbit:** 230,000u (outermost — beyond normal system charts)

**Lore:**
Nox Prime doesn't appear on standard system charts. It shows up on the player's navigation computer only after they've spoken to Elder Mira and unlocked the final lore revelation: the Nox manufactured the war. The planet itself is a contradiction — it should be frozen solid at this distance from the star, but it isn't. The surface temperature is mild. The sky is always dark. The light comes from below — from vents in the ground that pulse deep violet, illuminating absolutely nothing useful.

The ruins on Nox Prime are not ruins from war or neglect. They were built to look like ruins. Everything on this planet is a designed impression — architecture intended to communicate without saying anything true. There are no Nox here. Or the Nox are everywhere and choosing not to be visible. The player finds a single blinking light on a console in the deepest structure. If they press it, nothing happens. The achievement unlocks anyway.

This is the end of the lore. This planet is the answer. The answer is: the question was wrong.

**Surface feel:** The quietest place in the system. Dark ground that reflects the violet glow from below. Ancient structures that are somehow too clean to be truly old. A feeling that the planet is looking back. One blinking light.

**For AI art generation:**
- Top-down pixel art, ancient alien ruin aesthetic
- Near-black ground surface with faint violet-purple glow pulsing from ground cracks
- Architectural structures: smooth dark stone, organic in shape but with mathematical precision — too perfect to be natural
- No weathering, no dust accumulation — everything clean despite apparent age
- Glow vents in the ground: narrow fissures with deep violet light pulsing rhythmically
- Single console structure at the center: a plinth of dark crystal with one small teal blinking point
- Absolute silence communicated through emptiness — no environmental clutter, wide open space
- Sky: pure black with no stars (the star is too far, too faint)

**Key palette:** `#0A0012` (near-black ground) · `#2D0040` (shadow purple) · `#7B2D8B` (glow violet) · `#00CED1` (console blink teal) · `#1A0020` (structure dark) · `#4A0060` (vent glow)

**Stub reason (in-world):** Nothing. Silence. One blinking light.

## Notes for AI Image Generation

When generating planet sprites and environments:

1. **Consistency rule:** Each planet needs a "hero tile" generated first (main terrain tile). All subsequent tiles for that planet should use `image_edit` with the hero as reference to maintain style consistency.

2. **View:** All surface sprites are **top-down** (not isometric). Camera is directly overhead.

3. **Pixel art style:** 16×16 source tiles, crisp pixel edges, no anti-aliasing, no gradients (dithering only). Stardew Valley is the reference — not 8-bit NES, not modern HD.

4. **Transparent backgrounds** on all character and object sprites. Opaque tiles only for terrain tiles (these fill the grid completely).

5. **Generation order:** Mira first (fully built sprint). Dusk and Sorn next (stub with one NPC and one terrain tile each). Vex planets after. Nox last.

6. **Brief structure for every generation call:** Subject → Style line → View → Palette (hex codes) → Background → Negative prompt. See GROK_ART_INVOCATION.md for full brief contract.
